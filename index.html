The client-specific equivalence checking (CSEC) problem is that of determining whether a component update affects the overall system; the Seasick project aims to address this problem using a variety of (semi-)automated techniques.

Projects

Freud

Our first subproject, Freud, uses symbolic execution to compositionally summarize system components. Freud’s exploration is guided by patterns we observed in a study of real-world software. Thanks to this, Freud significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art.

Future

Moving forward we intend to develop new techniques that improve applicability and scalability in collaboration with our industrial partners. Research directions include interactive verification with proof-assistants, and reasoning in the theory of floating-point numbers.
